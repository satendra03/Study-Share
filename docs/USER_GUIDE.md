## Getting Started

### Creating Your Account

1. **Visit StudyShare**: Navigate to the StudyShare website
2. **Click "Sign Up"**: Located in the top-right corner
3. **Fill in your details**:
   - Full Name
   - College Email Address (must be a valid college email)
   - Password (minimum 8 characters, include uppercase, lowercase, and numbers)
   - Department
   - Semester (for students)
   - Roll Number (for students)
4. **Verify your email**: Check your inbox for a verification link
5. **Complete your profile**: Add additional information to personalize your experience

### Logging In

1. Click "Login" on the homepage
2. Enter your registered email and password
3. Click "Login" to access your dashboard

### First-Time Setup

After your first login:
- **Complete your profile**: Add a profile picture and bio
- **Set preferences**: Choose your notification settings
- **Explore subjects**: Browse available subjects in your department
- **Bookmark favorites**: Mark subjects you're currently studying

## For Students

### Finding Study Materials

#### Browse by Subject

1. Navigate to **"Browse Materials"** from the main menu
2. Select your **Department** from the dropdown
3. Choose your **Semester**
4. Click on a **Subject** to see all available materials
5. Use filters to narrow down results:
   - Material Type (Notes, PYQs, Slides, Others)
   - Sort by (Recent, Popular, Most Liked)

#### Using Search

1. Click the **Search bar** at the top of the page
2. Enter keywords related to your topic
3. Use **AI-powered search** for natural language queries:
   - "Find notes about binary search trees"
   - "Show me operating systems previous year questions"
4. Apply filters to refine results

#### AI Recommendations

1. Go to your **Dashboard**
2. View the **"Recommended for You"** section
3. Get personalized suggestions based on:
   - Your current semester
   - Subjects you follow
   - Materials you've previously liked
   - Your study patterns

### Downloading Materials

1. **Find the material** you want to download
2. **Click on the material** to view details
3. **Review the description** to ensure it's what you need
4. Click the **"Download"** button
5. The file will download to your device
6. **Like the material** if you find it helpful (helps other students)

### Uploading Materials

#### Preparing Your Upload

Before uploading, ensure:
- ✅ You have the right to share the material
- ✅ The file is in a supported format (PDF, DOCX, PPTX, TXT, ZIP)
- ✅ The file size is under 50MB
- ✅ The content is accurate and relevant
- ✅ The file is properly named

#### Upload Process

1. Click **"Upload Material"** from your dashboard
2. Fill in the required information:
   - **Title**: Clear, descriptive title (e.g., "Data Structures - Trees and Graphs Notes")
   - **Description**: Brief summary of the content (50-500 characters)
   - **Subject**: Select from dropdown
   - **Material Type**: Choose Notes, PYQ, Slides, or Other
   - **Semester**: Select appropriate semester
   - **Department**: Your department
   - **Tags**: Add relevant keywords (optional)
3. Click **"Choose File"** and select your document
4. Review all information
5. Click **"Upload"**
6. Wait for confirmation message

#### After Upload

- Your material will be sent for **admin review** (if moderation is enabled)
- You'll receive a notification when it's approved
- Once approved, it becomes available to all students
- You can track views, downloads, and likes from your profile

### Organizing Your Materials

#### My Uploads

- View all materials you've uploaded
- Track performance (views, downloads, likes)
- Edit or delete your uploads
- See approval status

#### My Likes

- Access all materials you've liked
- Quick reference for exam preparation
- Export list for offline access

#### Bookmarks

- Save materials for later
- Create custom collections
- Organize by subject or topic

### Interacting with Materials

#### Liking Materials

- Click the **👍 Like** button on helpful materials
- Your likes help boost visibility for other students
- Materials you like appear in "My Likes"

#### Reporting Issues

If you find inappropriate or incorrect materials:
1. Click the **"Report"** button
2. Select the reason:
   - Inappropriate content
   - Incorrect subject/semester
   - Copyright violation
   - Poor quality
3. Add additional details (optional)
4. Submit report

Admins will review and take appropriate action.

## For Faculty

### Sharing Course Materials

#### Why Share on StudyShare?

- Centralized platform for all your course materials
- Students have easy access to official resources
- Reduce email attachments and drive links
- Track student engagement with your materials
- Support students across multiple batches

#### Uploading Lecture Materials

1. Navigate to **"Upload Material"**
2. Mark upload as **"Faculty Verified"** (automatic for faculty accounts)
3. Fill in course details:
   - Course name and code
   - Lecture topic or module
   - Semester and department
4. Upload your file (PPT, PDF, DOCX)
5. Add any additional notes or instructions
6. Click **"Share with Students"**

#### Managing Your Uploads

**Faculty Dashboard Features:**
- View all your uploaded materials
- See student engagement metrics:
  - Number of downloads
  - Likes and feedback
  - View count
- Edit or update materials anytime
- Archive old semester materials
- Organize by course and semester

#### Best Practices for Faculty

1. **Consistent Naming**: Use clear, systematic file names
   - Example: "CS301_Lecture05_ProcessScheduling.pdf"

2. **Regular Updates**: Keep materials current
   - Update at the start of each semester
   - Add supplementary materials as needed

3. **Detailed Descriptions**: Help students understand content
   - Include module/chapter information
   - Mention key topics covered

4. **Organize by Modules**: Structure materials logically
   - Group related topics
   - Number files in sequence

5. **Use Tags**: Make materials discoverable
   - Add relevant keywords
   - Include topic tags

### Monitoring Student Access

- View download statistics
- Identify which materials are most helpful
- Get insights into student learning patterns
- Respond to student questions or feedback

## For Administrators

### Managing the Platform

#### User Management

**Viewing Users:**
1. Go to **"Admin Panel"** → **"Users"**
2. View all registered users
3. Filter by:
   - Role (Student, Faculty, Admin)
   - Department
   - Registration date
   - Active/Inactive status

**Managing User Roles:**
1. Search for a user
2. Click **"Edit User"**
3. Change role:
   - Student → Faculty (for newly hired faculty)
   - Student → Admin (for trusted moderators)
4. Save changes

**Removing Users:**
- Use with caution
- Review user activity before deletion
- Consider deactivation instead of deletion
- Notify user before removal (if appropriate)

#### Content Moderation

**Review Queue:**
1. Access **"Moderation Queue"**
2. View pending uploads in chronological order
3. For each material:
   - Review title and description
   - Check subject and semester assignment
   - Verify file type and size
   - Assess relevance and quality

**Approval Process:**
1. Click **"Review"** on a pending material
2. Preview the file (if possible)
3. Choose action:
   - ✅ **Approve**: Make available to students
   - ❌ **Reject**: Decline with reason
   - ⏸️ **Request Changes**: Ask uploader to modify

**Rejection Reasons:**
- Inappropriate content
- Wrong subject/semester
- Poor quality or incomplete
- Duplicate material
- Copyright concerns
- File format issues

#### Managing Subjects

**Adding New Subjects:**
1. Go to **"Admin Panel"** → **"Subjects"**
2. Click **"Add Subject"**
3. Fill in details:
   - Subject name
   - Subject code
   - Department
   - Semester
   - Credits
   - Description
   - Syllabus (optional)
4. Save subject

**Editing Subjects:**
- Update subject information
- Modify semester or department
- Add or remove faculty associations
- Update description or syllabus

**Removing Subjects:**
- Archive instead of delete (preserves materials)
- Only delete if no materials exist
- Notify faculty and students of changes

#### Platform Settings

**General Settings:**
- Site name and description
- Contact information
- Moderation policies (auto-approve or manual review)
- File upload limits
- Allowed file types

**Email Notifications:**
- Configure notification templates
- Set notification triggers
- Manage email frequency

**AI Features:**
- Enable/disable AI search
- Configure AI recommendation settings
- Manage Gemini API settings

#### Analytics & Reports

**Platform Statistics:**
- Total users (students, faculty, admins)
- Total materials uploaded
- Downloads per day/week/month
- Most popular subjects
- Most active users
- Storage usage

**Generating Reports:**
1. Go to **"Reports"**
2. Select report type:
   - User activity
   - Material statistics
   - Department-wise breakdown
3. Choose date range
4. Export as PDF or CSV

### Handling Reports

When users report materials:
1. Review the reported content
2. Check reporter's reason
3. Investigate validity
4. Take action:
   - Remove if inappropriate
   - Contact uploader for clarification
   - Dismiss if report is unfounded
5. Notify reporter of outcome

## Features Guide

### AI-Powered Search

**How It Works:**
- Uses Gemini AI to understand natural language queries
- Analyzes material content beyond just titles
- Provides contextual results

**Example Queries:**
- "Show me notes about linked lists and their applications"
- "Find previous year questions on thermodynamics"
- "I need help understanding database normalization"

**Tips for Better Results:**
- Be specific about your topic
- Mention the subject if searching across multiple subjects
- Use technical terms when applicable
- Include context (e.g., "for exam preparation")

### Smart Recommendations

**Personalization Factors:**
- Your enrolled subjects
- Materials you've liked previously
- Current semester and department
- Download history
- Study patterns

**Improving Recommendations:**
- Like materials you find helpful
- Update your profile with current semester
- Follow subjects you're studying
- Regularly interact with the platform

### Ranking System

**How Materials Are Ranked:**
1. **Likes**: Primary ranking factor
2. **Downloads**: Secondary factor
3. **Recency**: Newer materials get a boost
4. **Verification**: Faculty-verified materials rank higher
5. **Quality Score**: Based on user interactions

**Contributing to Rankings:**
- Like helpful materials (your opinion matters!)
- Download and use materials
- Report low-quality or incorrect materials

### Notifications

**Types of Notifications:**
- New materials in your subjects
- Upload approval/rejection
- Likes on your uploads
- Comments or feedback
- Platform announcements

**Managing Notifications:**
1. Go to **"Settings"** → **"Notifications"**
2. Choose notification preferences:
   - Email notifications (On/Off)
   - Push notifications (On/Off)
   - Frequency (Instant, Daily digest, Weekly digest)
3. Select which events trigger notifications
4. Save preferences

## Troubleshooting

### Login Issues

**Problem**: Can't log in
**Solutions**:
- Verify email and password are correct
- Check if Caps Lock is on
- Try password reset
- Clear browser cache and cookies
- Try a different browser
- Contact support if issue persists

**Problem**: Email verification not received
**Solutions**:
- Check spam/junk folder
- Wait 10-15 minutes for delivery
- Request new verification email
- Verify email address is correct
- Add noreply@studyshare.com to contacts

### Upload Issues

**Problem**: Upload fails
**Solutions**:
- Check file size (must be under 50MB)
- Verify file type is supported
- Check internet connection
- Try a different browser
- Compress large files
- Split large ZIP files

**Problem**: Upload stuck at "Processing"
**Solutions**:
- Wait a few minutes (large files take time)
- Refresh the page
- Don't close the browser during upload
- Check internet stability
- Try uploading during off-peak hours

### Download Issues

**Problem**: Download not starting
**Solutions**:
- Check browser download settings
- Disable download managers temporarily
- Try right-click → "Save As"
- Clear browser cache
- Check available storage space
- Try a different browser

**Problem**: Downloaded file won't open
**Solutions**:
- Verify you have the appropriate software (PDF reader, etc.)
- Check if file downloaded completely
- Try re-downloading
- Check file isn't corrupted
- Scan for viruses

### Search Issues

**Problem**: Search returns no results
**Solutions**:
- Try broader search terms
- Check spelling
- Remove filters
- Try searching by subject instead
- Use fewer keywords
- Browse manually if search fails

**Problem**: Irrelevant search results
**Solutions**:
- Be more specific in query
- Use quotation marks for exact phrases
- Apply subject/semester filters
- Use AI search for better context understanding
- Try different keywords

### General Issues

**Problem**: Page not loading
**Solutions**:
- Check internet connection
- Clear browser cache and cookies
- Try incognito/private mode
- Disable browser extensions
- Try different browser
- Check if site is down (status page)

**Problem**: Features not working
**Solutions**:
- Enable JavaScript in browser
- Update browser to latest version
- Check if logged in
- Clear cache and cookies
- Disable ad blockers
- Try different device

## FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: Is StudyShare free to use?**  
A: Yes, StudyShare is completely free for all students and faculty.

**Q: Who can access StudyShare?**  
A: Currently, only students and faculty with valid college email addresses can register.

**Q: Can I access StudyShare on mobile?**  
A: Yes, the website is fully responsive. A dedicated mobile app is planned for future release.

**Q: How is my data protected?**  
A: We use industry-standard encryption, secure authentication, and follow data protection best practices.

### Account Questions

**Q: Can I change my email address?**  
A: Contact support to change your registered email address.

**Q: How do I delete my account?**  
A: Go to Settings → Account → Delete Account. Note that this is permanent.

**Q: Can I have multiple accounts?**  
A: No, one account per person. Multiple accounts may result in suspension.

**Q: I forgot my password. What should I do?**  
A: Click "Forgot Password" on the login page and follow the instructions.

### Materials Questions

**Q: What file types can I upload?**  
A: PDF, DOCX, PPTX, TXT, and ZIP files are supported.

**Q: What's the maximum file size?**  
A: 50MB per file.

**Q: How long does approval take?**  
A: Typically 24-48 hours, depending on admin availability.

**Q: Can I edit materials after uploading?**  
A: Yes, you can edit title, description, and tags. To replace the file, delete and re-upload.

**Q: Why was my upload rejected?**  
A: Common reasons include wrong subject/semester, poor quality, inappropriate content, or copyright issues. Check the rejection reason in your notifications.

**Q: Can I download materials for offline use?**  
A: Yes, all materials can be downloaded for personal study use.

**Q: Are downloaded materials safe?**  
A: We scan uploads for malware, but always use antivirus software on your device.

### Academic Questions

**Q: Can I use materials for assignments?**  
A: Materials are for reference and learning. Always follow your institution's academic integrity policy.

**Q: Who owns the materials I upload?**  
A: You retain ownership. By uploading, you grant StudyShare permission to distribute on the platform.

**Q: Can I share materials from StudyShare elsewhere?**  
A: Respect copyright and creator rights. Share links to StudyShare instead of redistributing files.

**Q: Are faculty materials official?**  
A: Faculty-verified materials are official course resources. Always verify with your instructor.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Which browsers are supported?**  
A: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge (latest versions recommended).

**Q: Why can't I see some features?**  
A: Some features are role-specific (students vs. faculty vs. admin).

**Q: Is there an API available?**  
A: Yes, see our [API Documentation](API.md) for developers.

**Q: Can I suggest new features?**  
A: Absolutely! Use the feedback form or GitHub issues.
